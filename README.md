# DSA 210 Project Proposal: Economic Spillover Effects of Wars on Neighboring Countries

## Overview

This project will examine whether wars create measurable economic effects on neighboring countries. Instead of focusing only on the countries where conflicts take place, I want to analyze how nearby countries may also be affected through refugee movements, regional instability, and disruptions in trade and economic activity. In this project, I will combine conflict data, economic indicators, and refugee-related data to explore whether neighboring countries show visible economic changes during or after major wars.

---

## Goal

The goal of this project is to understand whether wars in one country are associated with changes in the economic performance of neighboring countries. I aim to examine whether indicators such as GDP growth, inflation, and trade-related variables show noticeable changes during nearby conflict periods, and whether refugee inflows help explain part of these effects.

---

## Motivation

I want to work on this topic because wars in neighboring countries do not remain limited to the country where the conflict takes place. As someone living in this region, I think conflicts around us can also affect nearby countries economically and socially. For example, the Syrian war had effects beyond Syria itself, and neighboring countries experienced refugee inflows and different kinds of economic pressure. More recent conflicts in nearby countries made me think about this issue again. That is why I want to study whether wars create measurable economic spillover effects on surrounding countries.

---

## Data to be Used

### Conflict Data

I will use a public conflict dataset to identify major wars, their timing, and their intensity. This dataset will help me determine which countries are experiencing war and which neighboring countries may be affected.

### Economic Indicators

To measure the economic effects on neighboring countries, I will use public country-level datasets including variables such as GDP growth, inflation, and trade-related indicators.

### Refugee Data

Since refugee movements may be one of the main ways in which wars affect neighboring countries, I will also include refugee inflow data where available. I will use this data as an additional factor to better understand whether countries facing stronger refugee pressure also experience stronger economic effects.

---

## Analysis Plan

First, I will select several major war cases from different periods and identify their neighboring countries. Then, I will combine conflict, economic, and refugee-related data into one dataset. After cleaning and organizing the data, I will perform exploratory data analysis and visualizations to examine whether neighboring countries show economic changes during conflict periods. I will also apply statistical analysis to test whether these changes are significant and whether refugee inflows are associated with stronger economic effects.

---

## Expected Outcome

I expect that wars may have measurable spillover effects on neighboring countries, especially in indicators such as inflation, trade, and economic growth. I also expect that countries receiving larger refugee inflows may show stronger economic effects than countries that are geographically close but experience less direct pressure. The project may also show that the scale of the effect differs depending on the intensity and duration of the conflict.
